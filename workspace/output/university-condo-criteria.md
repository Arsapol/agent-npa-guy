# NPA University Condo Investment Criteria

## Team Discussion Summary

**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Context:** Building criteria for finding NPA condos near universities (within 800m) in Bangkok metro area for profit (flip or rental income).

### Round 1 Lessons Learned

- Many NPA properties are priced AT or ABOVE market (Park 24, Lumpini 24, Clover Ladprao 83, Studio Zone, The Selected Kaset — all had zero discount)
- Provider "appraised values" are marketing, not real market prices — never anchor to them
- Old buildings (1994 Monterey Place, 1997 รื่นรมย์/T.N.B.) have terrible liquidity even if cheap
- Triple Y Residence สามย่าน near จุฬาฯ is LEASEHOLD expiring 2049 — looked great until title check
- Some providers don't have GPS coordinates (LED has zero)
- Building age, developer brand, and active rental market matter hugely for both flip and rental strategies

## Team Members

### 1. Flipper (NPA Flip Investor — 50+ deals)

**Focus:** Buy-renovate-resell margins, exit speed, buyer pool sizing.

#### Top 10 Criteria

**Criterion 1: Real Market Discount ≥ 20% (NOT vs. Appraisal)**
- Rule: NPA price must be ≤ 80% of current DDProperty/Hipflat median for the same building, same size tier.
- Why: Renovation eats 8-15%, carrying costs 3-5%, transfer fees/taxes 3-4%. You need 20%+ just to break even. 25%+ to actually profit.
- Ignore at your peril: Park 24 — listed near appraised value but actual transaction prices were 110-115K THB/sqm. "20% below appraisal" was actually at market. Buyer was trapped.

**Criterion 2: Building Age ≤ 15 Years**
- Rule: Completed year must be 2011 or later. Hard cutoff. No exceptions for price.
- Why: Post-2011 buildings have keycard lifts, CCTV, modern lobbies, fiber internet. Pre-2000 buildings trigger buyer financing problems — banks appraise old buildings conservatively, cap LTV at 70%, killing 80% of your buyer pool.
- Ignore at your peril: 1997 condo near Kasetsart at 25K/sqm vs market 45K/sqm. But Kasikorn/SCB cap LTV at 70% on pre-2000 buildings. Buyer needs 30% cash down. Flip margin evaporates.

**Criterion 3: Unit Size 22-35 sqm Only**
- Rule: Target only studio/1BR in 22-35 sqm band. Skip below 22 sqm (financing issues) or above 45 sqm (wrong buyer for university corridor).
- Why: Near universities, buyers are parents or young professionals. Both maximize location, minimize size. 28-33 sqm hits "real unit" psychology while keeping price accessible. Rents in 1 week vs 6+ weeks for 2BR.
- Ignore at your peril: 65 sqm 2BR near Chulalongkorn — buyer pool is couples, not students. Carried 9 months, sold at 5% below buy price.

**Criterion 4: BTS/MRT ≤ 600m (Trumps University Distance)**
- Rule: BTS/MRT proximity ≤ 600m is mandatory. University proximity is a bonus, not primary.
- Why: Exit liquidity comes from transit, not universities. Universities can relocate faculties, change enrollment, go online. BTS lines are permanent. BTS-adjacent unit has buyer pool of entire Bangkok, not just parents/students.
- Ignore at your peril: Condo near Siam University (Petchkasem) — zero BTS within 1km. Resale took 14 months. Seasonal rental. Annual yield dropped from 6% to 3.8%.

**Criterion 5: Minimum 50 Active Listings in Building on DDProperty**
- Rule: If fewer than 50 active listings (sale + rent combined), skip. If fewer than 10 sale listings, skip regardless of price.
- Why: Thin listing count = thin liquidity. High count means brand recognition, established price benchmark, agents who know the building.
- Ignore at your peril: Studio Zone Ratchada — almost no DDProperty listings. Resale agent spent 3 months explaining the building. Discounted 8% to close.

**Criterion 6: Renovation Budget Cap at 12% of Purchase Price**
- Rule: If renovation exceeds 12% of purchase price, reject. Budget 3,000-4,500 THB/sqm for cosmetic flip. Structural issues = walk away.
- Why: NPA sweet spot is "needs cosmetic refresh" not "gut renovation." At 12% cap, spend ~120K on 1M unit, recover 150-180K in resale premium.
- Ignore at your peril: NPA near Mahidol Salaya — water-stained ceilings assumed 80K fix. Reality: roof membrane failure, 280K + 4 months. At market price with 7-month carrying cost.

**Criterion 7: Juristic Fee Arrears ≤ 24 Months**
- Rule: Request ค่าส่วนกลางค้างชำระ before bidding. Max 24 months. This amount comes off profit. Beyond 24 months, demand price reduction or skip.
- Why: Condominium Act Section 18 makes buyer responsible for up to 5 years of unpaid fees. 30 sqm at 45 THB/sqm/mo, 5 years = 81K surprise cost.
- Ignore at your peril: BAM Ladprao unit — 22% discount looked great. 4.5 years arrears = 97K + 1.5% penalty = 128K surprise. 22% became 9% effective.

**Criterion 8: Verified GPS + Satellite Ground-Truth**
- Rule: Every candidate must have verified GPS. Cross-check on Google Satellite AND Street View. LED has zero GPS — must reverse-geocode.
- Why: Can buy thinking 400m from BTS and discover 1.4km away. Price difference: 25-40% in resale value.
- Ignore at your peril: KTB "Sukhumvit area, near BTS" — actual coordinates: 1.1km from Udom Suk, behind industrial estate. Exit price: 35% below paid.

**Criterion 9: University Must Be Top-20 or 15,000+ Students**
- Rule: Target buildings near universities with ≥ 15,000 students OR top-20 ranked. Vocational/small private colleges don't create sustained demand.
- Why: Small colleges have volatile enrollment, shift to online. Large flagships have 40-year track records. 500m from Chula = guaranteed rental queue every August.
- Ignore at your peril: Mid-tier private college in Nonthaburi shifted 40% online. Enrollment 8K → 4.5K. Rental demand collapsed. Loss: 18% on exit.

**Criterion 10: Minimum 3 Comparable Transactions in Same Building in Past 18 Months**
- Rule: Verify at least 3 closed transactions (not listings) in same building within 18 months. Sources: Land Department, agent networks, DDProperty sold data.
- Why: Listings prove supply. Transactions prove demand. Zero history = you're the price discovery experiment.
- Ignore at your peril: JAM condo near ABAC, 200-unit building. No transaction data. Three other NPA units listed simultaneously. Four sellers, zero buyers. Cut 12% to exit first.

**Flipper Summary Matrix:**

| Criterion | Hard Cutoff | Ideal |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Real discount vs market | < 20% = reject | 25-30% |
| Building age | > 15 years = reject | 5-12 years |
| Unit size | < 22 or > 45 sqm = skip | 26-33 sqm |
| BTS/MRT distance | > 800m = reject | < 400m |
| DDProperty listings | < 10 sale = skip | 50+ |
| Renovation cost | > 12% of purchase = reject | 6-9% |
| Juristic arrears | > 24 months = renegotiate | 0-12 months |
| GPS verified | Unverified = hold | Ground-truthed |
| University size | < 15K students = skip | > 25K |
| Transaction history | 0 in 18 months = -10% buffer | 5+ |

**Non-negotiables:** Criteria 1 (discount), 2 (age), 4 (BTS). Everything else can be priced in.

### 2. Rental Portfolio Manager (200+ units near universities)

**Focus:** Tenant demand, occupancy rates, yield reality, seasonal patterns.

#### Top 10 Criteria

**Criterion 1: Walking Distance to University Gate (Max 800m, Sweet Spot 400m)**
- Rule: Units within 400m get 15-20% rent premium. Reject beyond 800m unless BTS < 300m AND campus shuttle exists.
- Why: At Ramkhamhaeng, 400m band: 7-9K/mo. At 700m: 5.5-6.5K. Premium evaporates fast.
- Ignore at your peril: 750m = competing with regular market supply, not captive university premium. Occupancy drops 95% → 70%.

**Criterion 2: Building Age — Hard Cutoff at 2000, Preference Post-2005**
- Rule: Reject pre-1995 outright. 1995-2000 needs verified lift/plumbing/management. Post-2005 preferred; post-2010 optimal.
- Why: Students care about working lifts, drainage, electrical capacity (AC + laptop + phone + TV). A 1994 building fights systemic problems. Juristic office quality degrades sharply pre-2000.
- Ignore at your peril: 1997 unit at 380K. Lift breaks 3 weeks → tenant leaves. Pipes leak → liable. Common fund depleted → assessments. 8% yield → 3% after capex.

**Criterion 3: Unit Size — 24-35 sqm Studios, 35-50 sqm 1BR**
- Rule: Target 24-35 sqm studios OR 35-50 sqm 1BRs. Reject under 22 sqm or over 50 sqm.
- Why: 28 sqm = workhorse. At Kasetsart: 28 sqm = 267 THB/sqm/mo vs 60 sqm = 183 THB/sqm/mo. Smaller unit wins on yield every time.
- Ignore at your peril: 185 sqm NPA — target tenant is families, not students. Pool = zero. Short-term rental = legal complexity.

**Criterion 4: BTS/MRT — Secondary to University, Essential for Young Professionals**
- Rule: Pure student play: university walkability wins. Mixed play: BTS/MRT within 1.2km OR shuttle confirmed.
- Why: Undergrads live on-campus orbit. Young professionals commute to jobs. BTS access fills summer gap with year-round tenants.
- Ignore at your peril: Near Ramkhamhaeng (no BTS). June-August: 50% go home to province. Annual yield drops 12% from modeled 95% occupancy.

**Criterion 5: Essential Amenities — Non-Negotiable List**
- Rule: MUST HAVE: working lifts (1 per 50 units), 24hr security/keycard, CCTV, covered parking. PREFER: pool, gym, WiFi common areas. DEALBREAKER: non-functioning juristic office.
- Why: Keycard = #1 safety signal for female students (largest segment). No keycard = lose 50-60% of tenant pool. Pool/gym add 500-1K/mo rent.
- Ignore at your peril: No keycard building. Student's motorbike stolen. Word spreads in LINE groups within 48 hours. Vacancy runs 25%.

**Criterion 6: Monthly Rent Sweet Spot — 5,500-9,000 THB**
- Rule: Target achievable rent of 5,500-9,000 THB/mo. Above 10K = competing with young professional market. Below 5K = degraded tenant quality.
- Why: Thai middle-class family student allowances: 5-8K/mo. Government loan students (กยศ) have fixed ceilings. 7K studio with pool/gym/keycard rents in 2 weeks. At 11K, wait 6-8 weeks.
- Ignore at your peril: Market rent 13K, model great yield. 3 vacancies/year × 6 weeks = 4.5 months lost = effective yield collapses.

**Criterion 7: Furnished Standard — Full Furniture + White Goods Required**
- Rule: Deliver fully furnished: bed, wardrobe, desk+chair, TV, AC, fridge, washing machine. Unfurnished not viable.
- Why: Students arrive with nothing. Unfurnished rents 1.5-2.5K less/mo, takes 3-5x longer to fill. Furniture investment (60-90K for 28 sqm) pays back in 3-4 months of premium.
- Ignore at your peril: Save 70K unfurnished. Sits empty 3 months (-21K). Rents 1.5K/mo less permanently. One-time saving = permanent yield loss.

**Criterion 8: Minimum Yield — 7% Gross, 5.5% Net**
- Rule: Require 7%+ gross yield. After common fees, property tax, maintenance (3%), vacancy buffer (8%), furniture depreciation: need 7% gross → 5.5% net. Below 5.5% net = Thai bonds beat you.
- Why: University rentals have higher turnover, more wear and tear, summer vacancy. These are real, consistent costs.
- Ignore at your peril: Buy near CU at 3.2M, rent 15K/mo = 5.6% gross → 3.8% net. Fixed deposit would have been better.

**Criterion 9: Demand Verification — 3 of 5 Signals Required**
- Rule: Require at least 3: (1) 5+ active rentals in building on DDProperty, (2) Facebook uni housing group demand, (3) On-site 70%+ occupancy, (4) Juristic confirms 75%+ occupancy, (5) 2 agents confirm <4 week rental time.
- Why: NPA valuations can be 2-5 years old. A building strong in 2019 may have a 2022 competitor stealing tenants.
- Ignore at your peril: Buy on historical reputation. Arrive to find 45% occupancy — new 2021 building 200m away has pool+gym+shuttle at same price. 50% occupancy indefinitely.

**Criterion 10: Summer Vacancy Strategy**
- Rule: Accept summer vacancy only if: (a) mixed tenant base (grad + professional + staff), OR (b) own 5+ units to spread cost, OR (c) within 1km of both university AND BTS.
- Why: Thai academic calendar: mid-May to August + late Jan to March vacancy spikes. Pure undergrad buildings drop to 40-50% occupancy for 10-12 weeks/year. Single-unit landlords collect 8-9 months/year, not 12.
- Ignore at your peril: One unit, pure student, Rangsit corridor. 7.2% gross at full occupancy → 5.4% effective (9 months income). Below threshold every year.

**Rental Summary Priority:**

| Priority | Criterion | Hard Cutoff? |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Distance to university ≤ 800m | Yes |
| 2 | Rental yield ≥ 7% gross | Yes |
| 3 | Demand verified (3+ signals) | Yes |
| 4 | Building age post-2000 | Soft |
| 5 | Unit size 24-50 sqm | Yes |
| 6 | Rent in 5,500-9,000 THB band | Soft |
| 7 | Full furnished delivery | Operational |
| 8 | Essential amenities (keycard, lift) | Yes |
| 9 | Summer vacancy mitigation | Yes |
| 10 | BTS/MRT within 1.2km | Soft |

**Three absolute killers:** Wrong location (beyond 800m), unverified demand (assumption-based), summer vacancy with no mitigation plan.

### 3. Risk Analyst (Due Diligence Specialist)

**Focus:** Legal traps, hidden costs, deal-killers, worst-case scenarios.

#### Top 10 Risk Checks

**Risk Check 1: Leasehold Title with Sub-30-Year Remaining Term**
- Rule: Auto-reject สิทธิการเช่า with less than 30 years remaining. Minimum: 35+ years if resell within 10 years.
- Why: Banks won't lend on leasehold condos. Buyer pool = cash-only investors demanding 40-60% discounts. Terminal value = 0 baht.
- Horror story: Triple Y Residence สามย่าน. Leasehold expires 2049. "50% discount" was actually fair value for declining lease. 12 years later, 17 years left, no bank will touch it. Net loss: ~1.2M.

**Risk Check 2: Juristic Person Fund Solvency**
- Rule: Request นิติบุคคลอาคารชุด financial statement. Red flags: sinking fund < 50%, arrears from > 15% of units, unpaid utility bills. Minimum: sinking fund ≥ 70% funded.
- Why: You inherit the building's condition. Broken elevators, unpaid security, disconnected pumps. If > 30% units NPA/vacant, remaining owners fund everything — special assessments 50-300K/unit.
- Horror story: 60-unit building near รามคำแหง, 22 units went NPA. Fund collapsed. Lifts failed inspection → walk-up. Special assessment: 180K/unit. NPA buyer's 40% "savings" paid back in year one.

**Risk Check 3: Outstanding Juristic and Utility Debt**
- Rule: Demand หนังสือรับรองปลอดหนี้ before transfer. If unavailable, estimate 24-60 months unpaid fees as hidden cost. Budget: monthly fee × 36 months minimum as contingency.
- Why: Under Section 29, juristic fees are a lien on the unit, not the person. New owner becomes liable.
- Horror story: LED auction Lumpini สุขุมวิท 77. Won at 1.2M. Juristic bill: 38 months × 3.5K = 133K + penalty = 157K. Effective price: 1.36M — above appraisal.

**Risk Check 4: Physical Building Age and Structural Status**
- Rule: Buildings > 25 years require structural survey (15-30K baht) before bidding. Auto-reject: never renovated, pre-1997 post-tension slab, visible spalling on columns.
- Why: Pre-1997 condos built under weaker codes. After 25-30 years in Bangkok humidity: rebar corrosion, spalling. Condemned building notice = rental income ends immediately.
- Horror story: 1994 building ลาดพร้าว. Bought NPA at 800K. กรมโยธาธิการ issued partial suspension after cracks in transfer beams. Insurance refused. 3 years stuck. Exit: 400K to demolition buyer.

**Risk Check 5: Title Deed Type**
- Rule: Only accept โฉนดที่ดิน (นส.4จ or นส.4ก) as underlying land title. Reject: นส.3, นส.3ก, สค.1, ภบท.5. Verify at district Land Office.
- Why: นส.3 has no precise GPS boundaries. Disputes can void entire project's land title. Banks refuse mortgages.
- Horror story: Small condo มีนบุรี on นส.3ก land. Adjacent factory disputed boundary. Land Department froze all transfers for 4 years. Buyer paid transfer tax but couldn't register as owner.

**Risk Check 6: Tenant Occupation — Eviction Timeline**
- Rule: If occupied, budget 6-18 months + lawyer 50-150K + court fees + storage 20-50K. Former owner = 12-24 months due to hardship extensions.
- Why: Thai courts give occupants multiple avenues to delay. Can't rent or renovate during proceedings.
- Horror story: NPA near เกษตรศาสตร์. Former owner's family (4 people) claims "caretaker" status. Separate civil suit required. 14 months legal, 120K lawyer, 42K carrying costs. First rental income: month 18. ROI collapsed from 8% to under 4%.

**Risk Check 7: Market Liquidity Test**
- Rule: Check DDProperty + Hipflat for project name. Minimum: 3+ active resale listings AND 1 completed transaction in past 12 months. If zero: apply 30% liquidity discount.
- Why: "No listings" means: everyone locked in below water, project legally frozen, or no secondary market at any price.
- Horror story: 8-unit สาทร boutique at 2.1M (vs 3.2M appraisal). Zero listings ever. Sale required personal network, took 11 months, closed at 1.75M. 350K loss.

**Risk Check 8: Appraised Value vs Actual Transaction Price**
- Rule: Never anchor to provider ราคาประเมิน. Pull 5+ actual closed transactions from กรมที่ดิน for same project/comparable within 500m, same tier, within 18 months.
- Why: Land department appraisals updated every 4 years. Bank appraisals were done at peak bubble. "50% off appraisal" can mean "5% above actual market."
- Horror story: Park 24 NPA unit. Appraised 4.8M. NPA at 3.1M (35% "discount"). Actual comps: 2.9-3.2M. Paid full market with NPA friction costs for zero benefit.

**Risk Check 9: NPA Concentration and CAM Fee Sustainability**
- Rule: Calculate (NPA units ÷ total units × 100). If > 20%, remaining owners carry disproportionate CAM burden. Check if CAM fee unchanged 5+ years — means fee suppression, large increase imminent.
- Why: When 20%+ units NPA, voting quorum fails, fee increases fail, building deteriorates while fees stay at 2015 levels.
- Horror story: Condo near ธรรมศาสตร์ รังสิต. 35% units NPA. CAM stuck at 25/sqm since 2018. Pool pump failed (350K), elevator motor burned (280K). Special assessment: 15K/unit. Rental income wiped for 5 months.

**Risk Check 10: University Enrollment Trend**
- Rule: Verify enrollment trajectory via กระทรวงศึกษาธิการ or university annual report. Reject if: > 10% decline over 3 years, shifting online, or new campus outside Bangkok planned.
- Why: Entire thesis is rental yield from students. If university shrinks/goes hybrid/builds dorms, tenant base evaporates. 22-sqm studios have zero alternative use.
- Horror story: Private university ปทุมธานี lost engineering accreditation. Enrollment dropped 28% in 2 years. Vacancy: 5% → 40%. Rents: 7K → 4.5K. Exit required 25% haircut.

**Risk Auto-Reject Matrix:**

|-------|-------------|----------|

| 5. Title deed type | Yes | None |
| 4. Building structure notice | Yes if condemned | None |
| 6. Tenant eviction | No — price it in | 6-18 months, 200K+ |
| 3. Juristic debt | No — price it in | 50-300K |
| 8. Appraised vs real price | No — recalculate | Walk away if margin < 20% |

### 4. Market Trend Analyst

**Focus:** Supply-demand dynamics, infrastructure, demographics, future value.

#### Top 10 Criteria

**Criterion 1: BTS/MRT + University Dual Proximity Required**
- Rule: Only target condos within 800m of university AND within 600m of operational BTS/MRT. Both conditions simultaneously.
- Why: University alone = rental demand. Transit = price appreciation. Dual-demand survives post-graduation. Single-driver locations are one enrollment dip from vacancy.
- What most investors miss: MRT Yellow Line (2023) repriced Lat Phrao-Ramkhamhaeng corridor. NPA pricing often still reflects pre-transit values.

**Criterion 2: University Enrollment Trajectory Filter**
- Rule: Reject corridors where anchor university has declining undergrad enrollment for 2+ consecutive years. Target stable/growing or strong international programs.
- Why: Thai private university enrollment structurally declining (birth rate collapse post-2000). ม.รังสิต, ABAC programs already saw 15-30% drops.
- What most investors miss: International student programs are demand wildcards. ABAC Hua Mak retains demand via English MBA/law programs drawing ASEAN students. 15%+ international student rate = demand floor.

**Criterion 3: NPA Concentration Ratio in Same Project**
- Rule: Reject projects where > 8% of units appear across all NPA databases combined. Flag 5-8%.
- Why: High concentration signals: underfunded juristic, systemic value decline, contagion effect.
- What most investors miss: Check across ALL providers using npa-adapter. BAM has 4, JAM has 3, KBank has 2 in same building — only visible when aggregated.

**Criterion 4: Year-Built Sweet Spot (2008-2018)**
- Rule: Target 2008-2018. Penalize pre-2005 (structural risk, no EV parking, no fiber) and post-2019 (oversupply era).
- Why: Old enough for stable juristic/sinking funds, cheap enough for NPA discount below replacement cost, modern enough for keycard/AC/amenities.
- What most investors miss: 2015-2018 subperiod is the sweet spot within the sweet spot. 7-10 year mark where major infrastructure hasn't yet needed replacement but priced as aging stock.

**Criterion 5: Supply Pipeline Check — New Projects Within 1km**
- Rule: Reject corridors with new construction adding 500+ units within 1km. Check EIA filings and local municipality permits.
- Why: New supply destroys pricing power 3-5 years. Ramkhamhaeng saw 8,000+ new units 2014-2019. Yields compressed from 6-7% to 4-5%. NPA volumes spiked.
- What most investors miss: Check EIA approvals, not just construction starts. Approved EIAs = shadow supply. Lat Phrao 71-101 has several approved sites not visible on ground.

**Criterion 6: Rental Price Compression Ratio**
- Rule: Target areas yielding 6.5%+ gross at current market rents. Discard below 5.5% even at optimistic rents.
- Why: Oversupplied corridors (Ramkhamhaeng, Din Daeng) compressed to 4-6K/mo for 28-35 sqm. Near Chula/SWU (supply-constrained), 30 sqm studios rent 8-12K — different yield math.
- What most investors miss: Rental ceiling near universities set by dorm alternatives. Chula dorms subsidized at 2.5-4.5K/mo. But grad students/faculty/professionals ignore dorm pricing. Target buildings where non-student pool ≥ 40%.

**Criterion 7: Infrastructure Forward-Look — Unpriced Future Transit**
- Rule: +1 tier bonus for properties within 600m of station on line under construction, completion within 36 months. Active civil works only, not planning stage.
- Why: NPA prices set today, transit value crystallizes at opening. Orange Line (Thailand Cultural Centre-Min Buri East) will reprice Ramkhamhaeng-Hua Mak corridor.
- What most investors miss: Orange Line western extension (Bang Khun Non-Thailand Cultural Centre) creates new corridor connecting ธรรมศาสตร์ ท่าพระจันทร์ and ศิลปากร. Current NPA pricing reflects pre-transit values.

**Criterion 8: Co-living/Dorm Substitution Risk**
- Rule: Disqualify corridors with 3+ purpose-built co-living operators within 1km in last 24 months.
- Why: Co-living structurally eating 6-10K/mo condo segment. Furnished units with utilities/cleaning/WiFi at competitive all-in prices.
- What most investors miss: Threat highest for 25-40 sqm studios at 7-12K — precisely NPA sweet spot. But spares 1BR 45sqm+ (privacy, personal appliances). Push toward 1BR over studios.

**Criterion 9: University-Type Demand Profile**
- Rule: Prioritize graduate/professional school corridors (MBA, law, medicine, engineering) over pure undergraduate mass-enrollment. Minimum: 10+ postgraduate programs.
- Why: Grad students have higher income, longer tenancy (2-3 years vs 1), lower price sensitivity. Pay 12-25K/mo without complaint. Undergrad-only institutions anchor price-sensitive, short-tenancy pool.
- What most investors miss: Hospital clusters adjacent to medical universities = permanent non-student demand. Siriraj Hospital: 12K+ staff prefer nearby condo rental. Enrollment-proof, recession-resistant.

**Criterion 10: Price Discount Range (30-55%)**
- Rule: Target NPA 30-55% below DDProperty/Hipflat median. Reject < 30% (insufficient margin) OR > 55% (hidden defects/encumbrances).
- Why: 30% floor: renovation headroom + holding cost buffer + 15% market correction cushion. 55% ceiling: extreme discounts almost always mean severe juristic debt, title disputes, eviction needs, or structural defects.
- What most investors miss: LED auction floor is 70% of appraisal by law. Appraisals often 15-25% below market. Effective LED discount = only 10-20%. SAM/BAM sometimes offer better effective discounts via negotiation.

## Key Disagreements Between Teammates

| Topic | Flipper | Rental | Risk | Market |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Minimum discount | 20% | — | — | 30% |
| Maximum discount | No cap | — | — | 55% (above = hidden defects) |
| Max building age | 15 years (2011+) | Post-2000 OK | 25 years needs survey | 2008-2018 sweet spot |
| BTS vs University priority | BTS trumps uni | Uni trumps for students | — | Both required |
| Target tenant | Parent-buyers / flip | Undergrad students | — | Grad/professional |
| Unit size | 22-35 sqm | 24-50 sqm | — | 1BR over studio (co-living risk) |

### Resolution

1. **Discount**: 20% minimum (Flipper floor), 30% ideal, flag > 55% for extra due diligence (Market's trap warning)
2. **Building age**: 2008-2018 sweet spot (Market consensus) — old enough for NPA discounts, new enough for renters
3. **BTS vs University/School**: Tiered approach (see below) — requiring both eliminates ~70% of candidates
4. **Target tenant**: Mixed buildings (student + young professional + families) solve summer vacancy problem
5. **Unit size**: 24-35 sqm studios for university yield, 35-45 sqm 1BR for co-living defense, 45-80 sqm 2BR for school/family segment

## BTS/MRT Tiered Approach (Revised)

Requiring BOTH BTS/MRT + education institution within 800m eliminates ~70% of candidates — too narrow. Instead, use BTS/MRT as a **yield threshold modifier**:

| Tier | Condition | Min Yield | Min Discount | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Education + BTS/MRT both < 800m | 6% | 15% | Low |
| **B** | Education < 800m, BTS/MRT 800-1500m | 7% | 20% | Medium |
| **C** | Education < 800m, no BTS/MRT within 1500m | **8%** | **25%** | Higher |

**Tier C additional requirements**: verified rental demand (3+ signals) + institution enrollment ≥ 15,000 students (or school with waitlist).

**Logic**: No BTS = harder exit + summer vacancy risk → demand higher yield and deeper discount to compensate. Market prices this naturally — no-BTS condos are cheaper, which is exactly why yields are higher.
