# International School Rental Demand Research
**Bangkok — Tier-1 International Schools**
**Date: 2026-04-04 | Purpose: NPA Condo Investment Evaluation**

---

## Methodology

Data sourced from: DDProperty, FazWaz, PropertyHub, Bangkok-Housing, Hipflat, RentHub, HeroRealtor, Nestopa, BangkokResidential market reports, ExpatSchoolGuide. All prices in THB/month unless noted. Exchange rate USD 1 ≈ THB 35.

## School-by-School Analysis

---

### 1. NIST International School — สุขุมวิท 15 (Asoke/Nana)

- **2BR rent range:** 48,000–85,000 THB/month (58–75 sqm on Sukhumvit 11–21 corridor; budget outlier at 30,000 for old 112 sqm Ruamjai Heights)
- **3BR rent range:** 80,000–130,000 THB/month (125+ sqm units near Soi 13–21)
- **Tenant profile:** Senior expat executives, diplomats, Japanese/Korean nationals on corporate packages. NIST attracts multinational company employees. Most on employer-paid housing allowances.
- **Demand signal:** STRONG — 4,027 rental listings near school (high volume but demand absorbs: Asoke-Phrom Phong rents in 15–25 days vs Bangkok avg 30–40 days)
- **Competition:** Heavy condo supply on Sukhumvit 15–21 corridor; multiple serviced apartment buildings (Grande Centre Point, Amari); no campus bus (location is central enough)
- **Key insight:** Highest-demand corridor in Bangkok; oversupply is offset by constantly renewing expat corporate relocations; premium pricing holds due to BTS Asoke + MRT Sukhumvit double interchange

---

### 2. Bangkok Patana School — สุขุมวิท 105 / ลาซาล (Bangna)

- **2BR rent range:** 20,000–40,000 THB/month (Bearing/LaSalle corridor; budget stock at 12,000–15,000 for dated buildings)
- **3BR rent range:** 35,000–65,000 THB/month (larger units near BTS Bearing)
- **Tenant profile:** Mix of expat families (especially British, Australian nationals) and upper-Thai families. BPA is the largest international school in Thailand (~2,600 students). Corporate housing less dominant than ISB/NIST; more self-paying senior expats.
- **Demand signal:** STRONG — Active school with waitlists; dedicated property ecosystem around LaSalle area; proximity to BTS Bearing and BTS On Nut creates strong mid-tier rental market
- **Competition:** School provides bus routes; reduces strict need to live on LaSalle. Competing serviced apts modest (not a major corporate housing hub). St Andrews shares BTS Bearing area creating combined school-catchment demand.
- **Key insight:** Lasalle/Bearing area is Bangkok's most established school-catchment rental zone outside Sukhumvit prime; strong British expat community creates durable demand; prices more moderate than Asoke making yields more attractive

---

### 3. Shrewsbury International School — เจริญนคร (Riverside)

- **2BR rent range:** 26,000–60,000 THB/month (Niche Mono Charoen Nakhon at 26K/50sqm to Chapter Charoen Nakhon Riverside at 60K/68sqm; Supalai Premier at 59,500)
- **3BR rent range:** 70,000–120,000 THB/month (estimated; limited Charoen Nakhon 3BR condo supply)
- **Tenant profile:** Affluent expats working in Silom/Sathorn CBD (5–10 min commute). School catchment draws Bang Rak, Sathorn, Charoen Krung corridor families. Mix of self-paying and corporate-paying.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE-STRONG — School's private shuttle boat from pier reduces strict proximity need; but riverside luxury condo market (ICONSIAM, Avani area) is maturing and supply is thin
- **Competition:** School operates shuttle boat service from dedicated pier (key differentiator — families can live further along riverside). Limited serviced apts in Charoen Nakhon itself. ICONSIAM mixed-use attracting non-school renters diluting school-specific demand.
- **Key insight:** Boutique high-demand school but limited condo supply in immediate 1km — most school families live in Sathorn/Silom 3–5km away; if buying near school, supply scarcity creates premium; but low liquidity risk

---

### 4. ISB — นิชดาธานี ปากเกร็ด (Nonthaburi)

- **2BR rent range:** 43,000–80,000 THB/month (Nichada Thani condos); houses dominate
- **3BR rent range:** 80,000–195,000 THB/month (Nichada Thani 3–4 bedroom detached/townhouses; cluster starts at 80K)
- **Tenant profile:** American-dominant expat families, NGO/embassy workers, senior multinational managers. Nichada Thani is a fully self-contained gated community — most ISB families live inside the compound. Housing almost entirely employer-subsidised (ISB itself provides a list of Nichada rental units).
- **Demand signal:** STRONG within Nichada but GEOGRAPHICALLY RESTRICTED — demand is almost entirely for Nichada Thani properties. Outside Nichada, demand drops sharply. Extremely car-dependent location (no BTS/MRT).
- **Competition:** No bus needed (school is inside gated compound). Main competitor is the compound's own rental pool. Very few condos — market is almost entirely houses. Serviced apartments non-existent in this zone.
- **Key insight:** NPA condo play is NOT viable here — no condo supply inside Nichada; outside Nichada demand is weak; product mismatch between NPA distressed condos and the house-rental market that ISB families need

---

### 5. Harrow International School — ดอนเมือง

- **2BR rent range:** 13,500–20,000 THB/month (Don Mueang/Vibhavadi Rangsit; Parkview Viphavadi 64sqm at 16,000; median Don Mueang condo rent is 8,059 overall)
- **3BR rent range:** 20,000–35,000 THB/month (limited supply; most families opt for townhouses 30,000–60,000)
- **Tenant profile:** Growing school (British curriculum); more self-paying and upper-Thai families than corporate expat. Don Mueang is not a corporate expat hub — most senior expats avoid this far-north location. FazWaz lists 30 houses for rent near Harrow; condo supply thin.
- **Demand signal:** WEAK-MODERATE — Thin condo supply; low expat concentration in Don Mueang; school has bus routes serving wider Bangkok. Rental market driven more by local upper-middle-class than expat housing allowances.
- **Competition:** School has bus service (key — reduces location premium). No premium serviced apartment ecosystem. Area dominated by airport-adjacent hotels and local residential.
- **Key insight:** Don Mueang is a yield-trap for NPA condos — prices are cheap but so are rents (low absolute returns); tenant quality lower; difficult to attract the school-family tenant profile; avoid unless pricing is extreme outlier

---

### 6. St Andrews International School — สุขุมวิท 107 / Dusit / Sathorn

**Sukhumvit 107 campus (primary residential demand driver):**
- **2BR rent range:** 20,000–35,000 THB/month (Samrong Nuea, Mueang Samut Prakan; budget studios from 10K; quality 2BR near BTS Bearing at 23,000–35,000)
- **3BR rent range:** 35,000–60,000 THB/month
- **Tenant profile:** British expat families; overlaps with Bangkok Patana catchment (both near BTS Bearing). Mix of self-paying and corporate. Dusit/Sathorn campuses serve different demographics but smaller.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE-STRONG — Bearing/Sukhumvit 107 area benefits from double school proximity (St Andrews + Bangkok Patana); co-located demand amplifies rental market
- **Competition:** School provides bus; BTS Bearing access reduces location lock-in. Area competes with BangNa/LaSalle product.
- **Key insight:** Sukhumvit 105–107 corridor (Bearing) is the best NPA hunting ground in Bangkok's southeast — two top British schools within 1km of each other creates sustained demand; moderate price point allows better yield math

---

### 7. Regent's International School — พัฒนาการ

- **2BR rent range:** 22,000–40,000 THB/month (The Tree Pattanakarn avg 2BR at 27,750; Arden Pattanakarn from 42,000; Suan Luang area avg USD 784/month ≈ 27,400 THB)
- **3BR rent range:** 55,000–90,000 THB/month (Suan Luang avg USD 2,099/month ≈ 73,500 THB)
- **Tenant profile:** Upper-Thai and Korean/Japanese expat mix. Pattanakarn is a wealthy Thai residential area — less of a pure expat school like ISB/NIST. Growing expat enrolment but Thai families dominate.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE — Only 12 condo units for rent near school on DDProperty (very thin supply and demand). Suan Luang/Pattanakarn is not a traditional expat rental hub.
- **Competition:** School has bus service. No serviced apartment ecosystem in Pattanakarn. Competition from landed houses (area has many moo ban developments).
- **Key insight:** Limited condo rental market near school (only 12 listings); buyers face liquidity risk; better suited for house investment than condo; moderate demand from affluent Thai families who can self-pay

---

### 8. Wells International School — On Nut สุขุมวิท 95

- **2BR rent range:** 25,000–45,000 THB/month (Q House Sukhumvit 79 at 26,000–35,000; Aspire Onnut Station at 36,000–45,000; 150–250m from On Nut BTS)
- **3BR rent range:** 45,000–75,000 THB/month
- **Tenant profile:** Mix of younger expats (digital nomads, young families), budget-conscious corporate expats. On Nut is "value Sukhumvit" — same BTS access as Thonglor/Asoke at 40–50% lower rent. Growing DTV visa holders adding non-school rental demand.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE — Wells is smaller/less prestigious than NIST/BPA; On Nut itself has strong rental demand but driven more by lifestyle (BTS access, Tesco Lotus, local vibe) than school proximity specifically
- **Competition:** On Nut BTS creates strong independent rental demand; school has no major bus network (campus is walkable from BTS On Nut). Significant new supply pipeline along Sukhumvit 93–101.
- **Key insight:** Good area for NPA condos on fundamentals (BTS On Nut, lifestyle, yield) — but rental demand is area-driven not school-specific; Wells alone insufficient to drive premium; hold thesis should be BTS proximity not school catchment

---

### 9. Anglo Singapore International School — สุขุมวิท 31

- **2BR rent range:** 40,000–80,000 THB/month (Sukhumvit 31 sits in Phrom Phong/Asoke prime zone; Asoke Place 2BR at 25,000 for budget; Millennium Residence 2BR at 58,000/90sqm)
- **3BR rent range:** 66,000–120,000 THB/month (Whizdom Inspire nearby at 66,000/116sqm; Phrom Phong 3BR corner at 30,000 for older stock)
- **Tenant profile:** Singapore-affiliated expats, regional business executives, Korean/Japanese corporate families. Sukhumvit 31 sits in one of Bangkok's most premium residential corridors (Phrom Phong BTS zone). High proportion of employer-paid housing.
- **Demand signal:** STRONG — Sukhumvit 15–35 is Bangkok's tightest premium rental market (15–25 day average vacancy). 31 Residence and Siamese Exclusive are within 1km. Strong independent demand from BTS Phrom Phong.
- **Competition:** Limited bus service (central location). Heavy competition from serviced apartments (Grande Centre Point, MODO, various Sukhumvit boutique serviced apts). High supply of quality condos in the zone.
- **Key insight:** Excellent fundamentals but NPA condos here likely priced out of distressed range — any NPA in Sukhumvit 31 area is already at a premium; upside limited; works as capital preservation not yield play

---

### 10. VERSO International School — บางนา (Bang Phli, Samut Prakan)

- **2BR rent range:** 20,000–50,000 THB/month (Bang Na district; avg 2BR Bang Na at 47,373 per market data; budget at 20,000 for older stock; Coast Bangkok starts 12,000–75,000)
- **3BR rent range:** 50,000–96,000 THB/month (Bang Na avg 3BR at 95,936; houses more common than condos for families)
- **Tenant profile:** Mixed — some expat families, but VERSO is newer and smaller. Located near Mega Bangna and Suvarnabhumi Airport (15 min drive) attracting aviation-sector expats and some diplomatic families. Area also serves Concordian and Berkeley school catchments.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE — Bang Phli area is further from Bangkok core; condo supply growing but not deep school-specific demand. Multi-school zone (Concordian + VERSO + Berkeley in same area) diversifies demand.
- **Competition:** School provides bus service; 15 min to Suvarnabhumi means airport-proximate workers may rent independent of school. Rising new condo supply in Bang Phli creates competition.
- **Key insight:** Multi-school catchment (VERSO + Concordian) in Bang Phli creates broader expat demand base; airport proximity adds non-school tenant pool; moderate NPA opportunity if price/sqm is deeply discounted; oversupply risk from new developments

---

### 11. Brighton College Bangkok — กรุงเทพกรีฑา (Krungthep Kreetha)

- **2BR rent range:** 30,000–50,000 THB/month (townhouses dominate; The Patio from 30,000; Villa Albero from 38,000; condos minimal in this area)
- **3BR rent range:** 50,000–80,000 THB/month (Setthasiri Krungthep Kreetha from 50,000; Bangkok Boulevard Rama 9–Srinakarin from 58,000; Nirvana Define at 60,000–80,000)
- **Tenant profile:** Affluent British expat families. Brighton College Bangkok is one of the most expensive international schools in Thailand — parents paying 800,000–1,200,000 THB/year tuition are likely to have significant housing budgets. Mix of corporate and high-net-worth self-paying.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE-STRONG for houses, WEAK for condos — the area around Krungthep Kreetha is almost entirely house/townhouse product; condo supply minimal; families prefer gated house communities (Setthasiri, Nirvana Define, Bangkok Boulevard)
- **Competition:** School has bus service. No serviced apartment ecosystem (not a corporate zone). Market dominated by house rentals in moo ban developments.
- **Key insight:** Wrong product type for NPA condos — Brighton families want houses not condos; if an NPA condo appeared here, it would likely be an outlier with limited tenant pool; the house market is where investment thesis lives

---

### 12. KIS International School — ประชาอุทิศ / Huai Khwang

- **2BR rent range:** 20,000–33,000 THB/month (Pracha Uthit/Huai Khwang area; specific data: 20,000 for 45sqm, 31,000 for XT Huaikhwang 53sqm, 33,000 for 66sqm; Atmoz Ratchada-Huai Khwang at 22,000)
- **3BR rent range:** 40,000–65,000 THB/month (estimated from market data; limited 3BR condo supply in area)
- **Tenant profile:** Korean expat families (Huai Khwang has a large Korean community — "Korea Town" on Ratchada). Also serves families commuting to CBD via MRT. Mix of corporate and self-paying mid-to-senior expats.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE-STRONG — 95 condo listings near KIS on DDProperty (healthier market than Regent's); Huai Khwang MRT station + Ratchada corridor creates independent rental demand; Korean community creates stable ethnic-expat tenant base
- **Competition:** School is inside Kesinee Ville gated estate (limited bus needed — walkable within estate). Area has serviced apartments along Ratchadaphisek. MRT Huai Khwang reduces location lock-in.
- **Key insight:** Best "hidden gem" school zone — strong Korean expat demand, MRT access, relatively unknown to mainstream investors so NPA condo prices may be discounted; Ratchada/Huai Khwang is undervalued vs Sukhumvit despite comparable expat demand quality

---

### 13. Concordian International School — บางนา กม.7 (Bang Kaew, Bang Phli)

- **2BR rent range:** 15,000–30,000 THB/month (area is dominated by budget stock; Lumpini Mega City Bangna at 6,000 for studio; Niche Mono Mega Space at 12,000/1BR; quality 2BR estimated 20,000–30,000)
- **3BR rent range:** 30,000–50,000 THB/month (Whizdom Forestias at 35,000/1BR suggests 3BR pricing in 50K+ range; houses more common)
- **Tenant profile:** Mixed: some expat families, but Concordian is smaller (~400 students). Located at Bangna km7 near Mega Bangna — attracts families in The Forestias, Vive Bangna developments. More affluent Thai families than pure expat.
- **Demand signal:** MODERATE — Limited active rental market; Concordian shares catchment area with VERSO (2km away) and other Bangna schools; thin condo supply near school
- **Competition:** School has bus service. Vive Bangna is a high-end development with house rentals (120,000/month) creating a parallel luxury market. Very limited expat serviced apartment supply.
- **Key insight:** Shares demand thesis with VERSO (multi-school Bangna catchment) but smaller independent demand driver; The Forestias mega-project (if completed) could transform the area — too speculative for current NPA investment thesis

## Investment Thesis Summary

### Tier 1 — Buy NPA condos here
**Bangkok Patana (LaSalle/Bearing) + St Andrews (Sukhumvit 107):**
- Best risk-adjusted opportunity: dual British school catchment, BTS Bearing access, moderate prices (20K–40K 2BR rents), growing expat community, established school ecosystem
- NPA condos in this zone at 40–50% discount to market = strong yield with durable demand

**KIS International (Huai Khwang/Ratchada):**
- Undervalued by mainstream market; Korean expat community is stable long-term tenant base; MRT Huai Khwang; 20K–33K 2BR rents with lower acquisition prices than Sukhumvit
- NPA discount here could be significant (less competition from non-distressed buyers)

### Tier 2 — Buy only at steep NPA discount
**NIST / Anglo Singapore (Sukhumvit 15–31):**
- Highest rents but highest acquisition prices; NPA discount may not be deep enough to create yield; better as capital gain play

**Wells (On Nut) + VERSO/Concordian (Bangna):**
- Area fundamentals decent; school premium is secondary; BTS/lifestyle drives demand more than school

### Tier 3 — Avoid for NPA condo thesis
**ISB (Nichada):** Wrong product type entirely
**Harrow (Don Mueang):** Too far north; weak expat base; rents too low
**Brighton (Krungthep Kreetha):** Families want houses, not condos
**Regent's (Pattanakarn):** Thin market (12 listings); low liquidity
