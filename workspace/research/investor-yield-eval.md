# Yield Investor Evaluation — 30 NPA Picks
**Role:** INVESTOR-YIELD | Conservative, 5-10yr hold, 4% NRY minimum after ALL costs
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Benchmark:** 4% NRY after CAM, land tax (0.02%), vacancy (10%), maintenance (5% of GRY)

---

## Evaluation Framework

Net Rental Yield (NRY) = GRY × 0.85 (vacancy 10% + maintenance 5%) minus CAM/sinking fund
- CAM: ฿35–60/sqm/mo for condos; ฿1,500–3,000/mo for houses
- Stress test: rent -15% → NRY still > 4%?
- Exit question: if rental market collapses, can I sell at cost?

## Key Themes from Yield Perspective

1. **Worker housing wins.** Sriracha industrial zone (บ่อวิน) KBank townhouses #4 and #5 are the highest-conviction yield plays. Yield 8–11% with stable industrial tenant base.

2. **Low entry condos in GREEN zones are safe.** The ฿1–1.3M KBank condo triad (ห้วยขวาง, จตุจักร, บางนา) offers 5–5.6% NRY with capped downside. Not exciting, but reliable.

3. **All land is NO for yield.** Without structure, land generates zero income. Building execution risk is too high for conservative 5-10yr hold strategy.

4. **Building age is a hard filter.** Picks #10 (30yr), Sammakorn 1990s estate, State Tower 26yr — these all enter their maintenance liability decade. Pass.

5. **Size matters for yield stability.** 25sqm studios have high turnover and seasonal gaps. 54–90sqm units attract family tenants who stay 2-3 years. Better NRY consistency.

6. **Bangkok CAM kills small condo yield.** ฿3,000–5,000/mo CAM on ฿1.8M condo at 6% GRY leaves thin NRY. KBank's ฿1–1.3M condos avoid this by having lower absolute CAM.
