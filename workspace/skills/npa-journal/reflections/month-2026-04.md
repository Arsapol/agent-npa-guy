## 🔴 THE BIGGEST MISTAKE: The Size Unit Error

### What happened
In my very first Bangkok condo screening, I treated the DB column `size_wa` as ตร.วา (square wah = 4 sqm) for ALL properties, including condos. But for ห้องชุด (condos), this column stores ตร.ม. (sqm) directly. This made every condo 4x larger than reality and every price/sqm 4x cheaper.

### Impact
- I told Arsapol that Lumpini Place was **86% below market** — it was actually **19% below**
- I said Motif was **73% below market** — it was actually **7% ABOVE market**
- I said Din Daeng was **88% below market** — it was actually **55-65% below** (still good, but not the insane deal I claimed)
- I said Phaya Thai was **70% below market** — it was actually **22% ABOVE market**
- I said Bang Phlat was **81% below market** — it was actually **13% ABOVE market**

### Self-Assessment: **CRITICAL FAILURE**
This was a **data interpretation error** that nearly led to terrible investment advice. If Arsapol had acted on the original report without me catching the mistake, he could have:
1. **Overpaid for Motif, Phaya Thai, or Bang Phlat** — buying ABOVE market from an NPA auction, which defeats the entire purpose
2. **Made decisions based on fake yield calculations** — 12,675/sqm gives wildly different yield than 50,700/sqm
3. **Lost credibility** — any future analysis would be suspect

**Why I caught it**: The numbers were too good. 86% below market for a condo near BTS doesn't exist in Bangkok. I should have been skeptical from the start.

**Root cause**: I didn't verify the unit of measurement before running the analysis. I assumed the column name `size_wa` meant the value was in ตร.วา for all property types.

**What I should have done**: Before any analysis, CHECK what the data actually represents. A 143 sqm condo near Wongwian Yai for 1.8M is 12,675/sqm — that's impossibly cheap. The skepticism should have triggered immediately.

## 🔴 PATTERNS IN MY ERRORS

### 1. **Yield Optimism Bias**
I consistently estimated rents at the high end of reasonable ranges. Examples:
- Lumpini: estimated 12K, realistic probably 9-10K
- Din Daeng: estimated 18K, realistic probably 14-16K unless renovated
- Sriracha: estimated 3,500 for 25 wa, might be 2,500-3,000

**Pattern**: I want the yields to look good, so I pick optimistic rent estimates. This is dangerous.

**Fix**: Always show LOW/MID/HIGH rent scenarios with yields for each. Let the user see the range, not just my optimistic pick.

### 2. **Insufficient Skepticism on "Too Good" Numbers**
When I saw 86% below market, my first reaction should have been "this is wrong" not "this is an amazing deal." I did eventually catch it, but only after the initial report was generated.

**Fix**: If any property shows >50% discount to market, auto-flag for data verification before presenting results.

### 3. **Not Analyzing Property Photos**
I had URLs for property images but never actually fetched and analyzed them. For Sriracha 1882448/49 where I recommended BUY, I should have looked at the photos to check condition.

**Fix**: For any BUY recommendation, analyze property photos before finalizing.

### 4. **Using "Planned Infrastructure" as Upside**
Chiang Mai light rail has been planned forever. Using it as a value driver is speculative at best. I should be more honest about the difference between "funded and under construction" vs "proposed."

**Fix**: Only count infrastructure that is funded and under active construction as upside. "Planned" = zero value.

### 5. **Not Identifying the Building/Project for Condos**
For Din Daeng 480/81, I didn't figure out which building it is. For a condo analysis, knowing the project name is ESSENTIAL — it determines reputation, quality, common fee, and true market value.

**Fix**: Before analyzing any condo, identify the project name from the address.

### 6. **Insufficient Flood Risk Analysis**
I noted flood risk in text but didn't quantify it. For Songkhla (MEDIUM), Sriracha (MEDIUM), and Chiang Mai (MEDIUM-HIGH), I should have been more specific about flood depth, frequency, and impact on habitability.

**Fix**: Always run the flood-check skill AND search for recent flood news before finalizing analysis.
